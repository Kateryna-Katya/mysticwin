Enter the Fallout

THE SHATTERED WORLD 

A Planet After
the Fallout

Civilization didn’t disappear — it fractured.
Cities became arenas, deserts glow at night, and radiation seeps through everything, making the ground feel alive.
Honestly, nowhere in this game feels safe - everything's just waiting to collapse or explode. Warning lights flicker, structures crumble, and the world itself feels like an enemy.

BATTLE SYSTEM

Seriously, Every Move You Make Matters

The grid?
That's basically your battlefield right there.
Alignments don’t just resolve — they react, chain, and alter what comes next.
Trust me, you can't just button-mash through this - one wrong move and you're screwed for the next three turns.

POWER SURGES

When the Radiation Goes Crazy

Out of nowhere, these power spikes just slam into the game and everything goes absolutely bonkers:

  • symbol behavior changes

  • visual intensity spikes

  • pacing accelerates briefly

I swear, when that happens, my screen gets so bright I actually squint - like the radiation's real or something.

PROGRESSION

Nothing's Free in This Wasteland

You've got to fight for every bit of progress in this game.
Each defeated enemy unlocks deeper layers of interaction and new challenges.
The world remembers what you’ve overcome — and responds accordingly.
